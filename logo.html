A crest that is

Concept

The mark is built as a deliberate paradox, and it is built entirely from straight lines and angles. On the surface it borrows the visual grammar of a baroque luxury maison — a crown, a laurel wreath, a shield, a metallic gold finish, a repeating monogram canvas, a motto, and a founding year set in Roman numerals. Underneath that surface it carries a second, quieter system: the crown's gems and the points of the monogram are nodes, joined by thin connecting lines into a small network. The angular geometry is the bridge between the two readings, because the same hard edges that make the crest look carved and authoritative also make it look like a system diagram, which mirrors my own field, Technological Systems Management.

Denotation

A gold heraldic crest: a zig-zag crown set with diamond gems, a laurel wreath drawn as straight angled leaves, a six-sided shield, and the overlapping initials J and B inside it. Diamond nodes sit on the crown and at three points of the monogram, linked by faint lines. A ribbon reads "EST·MMXXVI," and a pale JB pattern repeats across the background.

Connotation

The crown signals authority and plays on my nickname ("prince"); the laurel signals victory and honor; the shield and the wordmark "House of Juhyun Bae" signal heritage and a curated identity. The gold signals premium craft. But the deliberate excess is itself the argument: where minimalism claims that power is restraint, this mark insists on the opposite. And the angular nodes quietly reframe all of it as a system — ornament on the outside, structure within.

An anchor does not float free of meaning; the design fixes it. — after Stuart Hall, on anchorage

How it was built

Construction logic

The crest was drawn as vector geometry rather than generated as an image, so every element is deliberate. Every curve was removed on purpose: the crown is a single zig-zag path, the laurel is built from straight angled strokes, and the frame is a hard six-sided shield instead of an oval. The crown's diamond gems share the same shape and size as the three monogram nodes, which is what lets the eye connect them into one network. The initials use a high-contrast serif so the gold gradient catches a metallic sheen from light to dark.

Scaling strategy

Baroque detail does not survive shrinking, so the mark steps down deliberately. The full crest is used large; a crowned monogram is used at medium size; and a solid two-letter icon is used for the favicon, where gradients and thin lines would otherwise turn to mud.

Why it works

The mark satisfies the core logo criteria. It is clear at scale because it reduces to a clean JB icon. It works in color and in black and white, since the monochrome monogram keeps the J solid and the B outlined for contrast. And it carries a genuine denotation–connotation relationship: the same angular shapes can be read as a baroque crest or as a system diagram, which is exactly the kind of layered, intentional meaning visual rhetoric asks for.
