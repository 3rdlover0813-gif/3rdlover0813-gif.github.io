Concept

The mark is a deliberate paradox built entirely from straight lines and angles. On the surface it borrows the grammar of a baroque luxury house — a crown, a laurel wreath, a shield, a metallic gold finish, a repeating monogram canvas, and a founding year in Roman numerals. Underneath, the crown's gems and the monogram's points are nodes joined by thin lines into a small network. The angular geometry is the bridge: the same hard edges that look carved and authoritative also read as a system diagram, mirroring my field, Technological Systems Management.

Denotation

A gold heraldic crest: a zig-zag crown set with diamond gems, a laurel wreath of straight angled leaves, a six-sided shield, and the overlapping initials J and B. Diamond nodes sit on the crown and at three points of the monogram, linked by faint lines. A ribbon reads "EST·MMXXVI."

Connotation

The crown signals authority and plays on my nickname ("prince"); the laurel signals honor; the shield and motto signal a curated house; the gold signals craft. But the deliberate excess is itself the argument — where minimalism claims power is restraint, this mark insists on the opposite, while the angular nodes quietly reframe it all as a system.

Prompt 1 · Full crest
A luxury heraldic crest emblem, strictly geometric and angular with no curves, gold metallic gradient on deep black background. A zigzag five-point crown set with diamond gems, flanked by two symmetrical laurel wreaths of straight angular leaves, surrounding a hexagonal shield. Inside, an elegant high-contrast serif monogram of "J" and "B" overlapping, with small diamond nodes connected by thin lines. A banner ribbon below reads "EST·MMXXVI". Faint repeating JB monogram pattern in the background. Symmetrical, vector logo style, flat, baroque maximalism, gold foil on black, 2px clean strokes.
Prompt 2 · Crown silhouette
Build the crown as five rising points on a flat band, drawn only with straight lines and miter joins — no curves — so the silhouette also reads as a system diagram. Strictly symmetrical, with diamond-shaped gems on the points.
Prompt 3 · Laurel & shield
Frame the monogram with a six-sided hexagonal shield and two symmetrical laurel wreaths whose leaves are sharp angular strokes rather than soft curves. Gold gradient, thin even line weight throughout.
Prompt 4 · Large / color variant
Full-color version: gold metallic crown and serif "JB" monogram on a deep black background, with the wordmark "HOUSE OF JUHYUN BAE" beneath in spaced serif capitals.
Prompt 5 · Mono & icon variants
Two reductions: (a) a black-and-white print version, serif "JB" with the J solid and the B as an outline on white; (b) a small icon, gold serif "JB" centered on a dark rounded-square badge, favicon style.

Why this passes Module 8

Clarity at scale. The mark reduces to a clean JB icon whose straight strokes survive downscaling to a 16-pixel favicon. Color and B&W both work, because the composition relies on shape, not on the gold; strip the color and a legible monogram remains. Denotation and connotation both hold: the same angular shapes can be read as a baroque crest or as a system diagram, with a motto and ribbon that fix the meaning the way Stuart Hall describes an anchor fixing the meaning of an image.
