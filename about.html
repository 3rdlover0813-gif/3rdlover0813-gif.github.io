About the author

I am a freshman in Technological Systems Management (TSM) at SUNY Korea, drawn to the seam where engineering, mathematics, and human systems meet. That interest is also why a visual-rhetoric course matters to me: an interface, a logo, and a video are all systems that persuade. An earlier project of mine looked at AI-based traffic-signal optimization for urban congestion, tied to the UN Sustainable Development Goals on industry & infrastructure (SDG 9) and climate action (SDG 13). I am considering a mathematics specialization later in the program.

What this site contains

This is the individual website deliverable for EST 240. It hosts three artifacts that are meant to be read together:

  1. Beyond the Headline — the research essay analyzing the visual meaning-making practices of the Indigo Traveller YouTube channel, in APA format with a full reference list.
  2. Maison JB — the personal mark, drawn as scalable vector geometry in three sizes (including a black-and-white variant) with a denotation–connotation defense.
  3. The site itself — hand-built to satisfy CARP (Contrast, Alignment, Repetition, Proximity) and CATN (Content, Aesthetics, Technology, Navigation).

Why Indigo Traveller

Among possible travel channels, Indigo Traveller is an unusually clear case for visual rhetoric. Host Nick Fisher films countries that Western media usually reduces to headlines — Somalia, North Korea, Iraq — and uses handheld camera work, slow editing, and a steady on-screen presence to argue, without ever stating it, that these places hold ordinary human lives. That gap between what the title promises and what the footage shows is exactly the denotation–connotation tension the course asks us to read, which made it a strong subject for the essay.

How the design meets the rubric

CARP — visual principles

Contrast
A dark ink background against warm off-white text; a display serif (Fraunces) set against a quieter body serif; gold reserved strictly for accents and the logo.
Alignment
A single centered reading column capped at 760 pixels; body text left-aligned; navigation and footer sharing one baseline across every page.
Repetition
The same nav bar, footer, type system, color tokens, and eyebrow label repeat on all four pages, so the site reads as one object.
Proximity
Essay metadata is grouped tightly under its title; logo size labels sit directly beneath each tile; references cluster at the foot of the essay.

CATN — production principles

Content
Each page carries one clear job: analysis, mark, or documentation. No filler.
Aesthetics
A consistent dark editorial system with a faint background grid, chosen to suit a course about how images make meaning.
Technology
Hand-written semantic HTML with a single shared stylesheet; the logo is SVG so it scales from favicon to hero without loss; the YouTube analysis embeds via the official iframe.
Navigation
A fixed top bar on every page; the personal logo links to this About page; the YouTube analysis lives on its own subpage rather than the front page.

Technology

The site is hand-coded in semantic HTML and CSS with no framework, hosted on GitHub Pages. One shared stylesheet drives every page, so a single change updates the whole site. The logo is drawn in SVG, which keeps it crisp from a 36-pixel favicon up to the full crest. The layout stays readable down to roughly a 360-pixel viewport.
